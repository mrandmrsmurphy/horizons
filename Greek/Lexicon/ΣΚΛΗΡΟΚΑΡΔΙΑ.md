1. Deuteronomy 10:16
1. Jeremiah 4:4

This is a very good example of the LXX translating the meaning, not the words, and the GNT continuing that trend.  The Torah translation of "Therefore circumcise the foreskin of your heart, and be no more stiff-necked." became "Therefore circumcise the hardness of your heart, and do not harden your neck."  Jeremiah echos the same words.  Matthew and Mark (in parallel) use this same, rare word to describe the Pharisees of Jesus' day.

Whoever invented the apocryphal ending to Mark was a well-read and intelligent person, artfully summing up all that had come before.  He includes this word too.
