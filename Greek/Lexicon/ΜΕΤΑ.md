preposition taking the Genitive and Accusative

1. With Genitive
## Association/Accompaniment: with, in company with
## Spatial: with, among
## Manner (Attendant Circumstance): with
1. With Accusative
## Temporal: after, behind
## Spatial (rare): after, behind
In earlier, Classical Greek, there was a usage with the Dative which meant
1. between,  among others,  but without the close union which belongs to the genitive,  of persons,  of things,  of separate parts of persons 
1.  to complete a number 
1. of collective nouns
but there are no such cases in our literature.

According to one source, there are a total of 473 uses in the GNT, with about 100 being with Accusative Case.  Also, μετά is greatly preferred for the meaning *with* over the preposition σὐν.
