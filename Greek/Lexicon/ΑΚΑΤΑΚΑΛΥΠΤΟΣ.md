This compound word is made up of NOT plus DOWN plus COVERED.  Though it has been translated as "unveiled" in the past, it would be better to say *unloosed*.  The Apostle Paul use of this adjective in Men, Women and Head Covering does not refer to being unveiled (an unknown custom among Greeks in that time) but to *loosed hair*.  In the Septuagint translation of Lev 13:45, (the only other time this word occurs) it refers to a man not putting his hair under a hat so that he may be marked as a leper.

## Usage
### NT
1. 1 Corinthians 11:5,13 - DSF modifying ΚΕΦΑΛΗ (head); ASF modifying ΓΥΝΗ (woman).

### LXX
1. Leviticus 13:45 - NSF modifying ΚΕΦΑΛΗ (head).

### Extra-biblical
- Philo uses the exact phrase of 1 Corinthians 11:5 (ἀκατακαλύπτῳ τῇ κεφαλῇ) clearly speaking of head covering being removed because the priest had just removed her kerchief. (*Special Laws* 3:60).  In *Allegorical Interpretation* II, 29 this word also means uncovered.
- Polybius 15,27.2 also uses this word to mean uncovered (100's B.C.)
- See also ΚΑΤΑΚΑΛΥΠΤΩ as this is a simple negative of that verb as an adjective.
