Dim. of κῶας

1. Nehemiah 3:15

Lightfoot says that the spring of Siloam discharged itself by a double stream into a twofold pool - the upper was called שילוח shiloach - the lower שלח shelach; the one signifying απεϚαλμενος, sent, the latter, κωδιων fleeces; and that our Lord marked this point so particularly, to inform the blind man that it was not to Shelach, but to Shiloach, that he must go to wash his eyes. These two pools seem to be referred to in Isaiah 7:23; Isaiah 22:9.

I cannot find the evidence for this, however.  I suspect that שֻׁלְחָן or שֶׁלַח are more likely sources of mistranslation, both of which were/are suspected to mean 'animal skin' in addition to their regular usages.
