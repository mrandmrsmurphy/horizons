Traditionally, this word has been translated "only-begotten", but, as we can see from Hebrews 11:17, this in inaccurate.  Abraham had more than one son, but Isaac was his *unique* son.  The first half of the word was always supposed to come from μονος (only), but the second half was assumed to come from γενναω (to beget).  Much more reasonable, however, is γενος (kind, sort).
