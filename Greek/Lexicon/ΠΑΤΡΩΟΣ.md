Grammarians distinguish πατρῷος, as expressing patrimonial possession, from πάτριος as expressing hereditary manners, customs, institutions ; v. Ammon. Diff. s. v., AB297, Suid., etc. — The distincion holds in Attic Prose ; but Homer and Herodotus use πατρώϊος only, and in all these senses ;
