Hapax
1. Acts 4:13

{{Quote| An older generation of scholarship tended to insist that Peter, a fisherman from Galilee labeled "unschooled" (ἀγράμματος) in Acts 4:13, would hardly have known Greek at all.  But recent research on languages in first-century Palestine has revelaed that Greek was widely used.|Carson/Moo, *An Introduction to the New Testament* (Zondervan, 2005), 642}}

The old idea of "unlearned" no longer holds water, since the disciples would have been passably trilingual (Aramaic, Greek, and Hebrew, with some knowing Latin).  It seems better to connect the γραμμ- part of this word with a γραμματεύς/Scribe, someone formally educated in the history of interpretation, either of the Pharisees or the Sadduccees.
