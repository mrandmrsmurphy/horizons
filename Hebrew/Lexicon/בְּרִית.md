**A relationship with obligations and promises.**

There has been a (total) failure to locate in another Semitic language an instance of a cognate root employed as a substantive in a covenant text.

- With Hebrew:כרת as the verb (to "cut" a covenant).

## Meaning
The safest conclusion at present if that of J. Guhrt<ref>*New International Dictionary of New Testament Theology*, i, 366</ref> who maintains that with the present state of the evidence there is no sure way to tell whether a conception is basic or whether it is derivative.  At the same time, we find it difficult not to agree with McCarthy to this extent: in so far as the available evidence is concerned, in a **ברית** the ideas of relationship (parties) and obligations is invariably present.  Unlike an oath, it must have parties, and upon one or the other or both obligations are always laid.
